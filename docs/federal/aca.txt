Accessible Canada Act (ACA)

Purpose:
The Accessible Canada Act (ACA) came into force on July 11, 2019. Its goal is to make Canada barrier-free by January 1, 2040, through the progressive identification, removal, and prevention of barriers.

Who It Applies To:
The ACA applies to federally regulated entities, including:
- Federal government departments and agencies
- Crown corporations
- Parliamentary entities (Parliament, Senate, Library of Parliament)
- Organizations in federally regulated sectors such as:
  - Banking and financial institutions
  - Telecommunications
  - Transportation (airlines, railways, interprovincial bus services)
  - Broadcasting

The ACA does NOT apply to:
- Provincial or territorial governments
- Private businesses that are not federally regulated
- Small businesses with fewer than 10 employees (exempted from some requirements)

Key Requirements:
Organizations covered by the ACA must:
1. Establish accessibility plans
2. Set up feedback processes
3. Report on implementation progress
4. Identify, remove, and prevent barriers in seven priority areas:
   - Employment
   - Built environment
   - Information and communication technologies (ICT)
   - Communication (other than ICT)
   - Procurement of goods, services, and facilities
   - Design and delivery of programs and services
   - Transportation

Standards:
The ACA authorizes the creation of accessibility standards. Organizations must comply with standards as they are developed and published.

Enforcement:
Accessibility Commissioner of Canada can:
- Conduct inspections and investigations
- Issue compliance orders
- Impose administrative monetary penalties for non-compliance

Timeline:
- Organizations with 100+ employees: Publish first accessibility plan by September 1, 2023
- Organizations with 10-99 employees: Publish first accessibility plan by June 1, 2024
