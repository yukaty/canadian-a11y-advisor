Provincial Accessibility Legislation - Summary by Province

Overview:
Provincial accessibility laws vary significantly across Canada. Some provinces have comprehensive legislation, while others have limited or no specific accessibility requirements for private sector organizations.

ONTARIO
Legislation: Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA), 2005
Status: Comprehensive accessibility law
Web Requirements: WCAG 2.0 Level AA (mandatory since January 1, 2021 for organizations with 50+ employees)
Applies To: All organizations with 1+ employees in Ontario
Key Features:
- Five accessibility standards (Customer Service, Information and Communications, Employment, Transportation, Design of Public Spaces)
- Multi-year accessibility plans required for 50+ employees
- Compliance reporting every 2-3 years
- Fines up to $100,000/day for non-compliance

MANITOBA
Legislation: Accessibility for Manitobans Act (AMA), 2013
Status: Developing accessibility standards
Web Requirements: WCAG 2.0 Level AA required for public sector websites
Standards Published:
- Customer Service Standard (2015)
- Employment Standard (2023)
- Information and Communication Standard (in development)
Applies To: Public sector organizations, requirements for private sector being phased in
Timeline: Working toward accessibility by 2025

NOVA SCOTIA
Legislation: Accessibility Act, 2017
Status: Framework legislation, standards in development
Web Requirements: WCAG 2.0 Level AA for government websites
Standards Published:
- Access to Goods and Services Standard (2018)
- Employment Standard (under development)
Applies To: Initially public sector, expanding to private sector
Timeline: Working toward accessibility by 2030

BRITISH COLUMBIA
Legislation: Accessible British Columbia Act, 2021
Status: Relatively new, standards in development
Web Requirements: WCAG 2.1 Level AA for government websites and services
Applies To: Initially prescribed organizations (government, public sector)
Standards: Being developed in areas similar to federal ACA
Timeline: First accessibility standards expected in 2024-2025

Web Requirements: No mandatory web accessibility requirements
Private Sector: No accessibility requirements unless federally regulated
Public Sector: Government services encouraged to follow WCAG but not legally required

SASKATCHEWAN
Legislation: The Accessible Saskatchewan Act, 2023
Status: Recently enacted, standards in development
Web Requirements: To be determined through accessibility standards
Applies To: Framework applies to public sector initially
Timeline: Standards being developed, implementation details forthcoming

OTHER PROVINCES AND TERRITORIES
Newfoundland and Labrador, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, Yukon, Northwest Territories, Nunavut:
- No comprehensive provincial/territorial accessibility legislation
- Some government policies encourage accessible websites
- Public sector may have internal standards
- Private sector has no legal requirements unless federally regulated

FEDERALLY REGULATED ORGANIZATIONS
Location: Anywhere in Canada
Legislation: Accessible Canada Act (ACA), 2019
Web Requirements: EN 301 549 (incorporates WCAG 2.1 Level AA)
Applies To: Banks, telecommunications, airlines, railways, broadcasting, federal government
Note: Federally regulated organizations follow federal law, not provincial law

KEY TAKEAWAYS BY PROVINCE

If you operate in Ontario:
- 50+ employees: Full AODA compliance required, including WCAG 2.0 AA
- 1-49 employees: Basic AODA compliance, WCAG 2.0 AA required by 2021

If you operate in Manitoba:
- Public sector: WCAG 2.0 AA required
- Private sector: Customer Service and Employment standards apply, web standards coming

If you operate in Nova Scotia or British Columbia:
- Standards being developed, monitor for updates
- Government and public sector have requirements
- Private sector requirements expanding

If you operate in Quebec, Alberta, Saskatchewan, or other provinces:
- No mandatory private sector web accessibility requirements
- Government websites may have standards
- Best practice: Follow WCAG 2.1 AA voluntarily

If you are federally regulated (anywhere in Canada):
- Follow Accessible Canada Act, not provincial law
- EN 301 549 (WCAG 2.1 AA) applies
- Accessibility plans and reporting required

Multi-Provincial Organizations:
- Must comply with laws in each province where you operate
- If operating in Ontario with 50+ employees, AODA applies to Ontario operations
- If federally regulated, ACA applies nationwide (provincial laws don't apply)
